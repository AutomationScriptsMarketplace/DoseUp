üî¨ Data Integration Methodology

Data Processing Pipeline

  1. Source Validation: All medical databases verified for accuracy and currency
  2. Data Normalization: Standardization using RxNorm and ATC classification systems
  3. Conflict Resolution: Priority-based system for conflicting information
  4. Clinical Review: Periodic review by licensed pharmacist consultants
  5. Version Control: Tracked updates with change logs and validation testing

Quality Assurance Process

  • Automated Testing: Continuous validation against reference databases
  • Clinical Validation: Expert review of interaction algorithms
  • User Feedback Integration: Healthcare provider and user experience feedback
  • Regulatory Compliance: FDA guidance alignment for mobile medical apps

Algorithm Transparency

  • Rule-Based Logic: No "black box" AI - all recommendations follow explicit rules
  • Traceable Sources: Every recommendation links to specific clinical evidence
  • Confidence Scoring: Recommendations include confidence levels based on evidence strength
  • User Override: Healthcare providers and patients can modify recommendations

‚ö†Ô∏è Limitations & Accuracy

üö® Important Limitations

  • Individual Variation: Recommendations are population-based, not personalized to individual metabolism
  • Clinical Context: Cannot account for specific medical conditions or comorbidities
  • Prescriber Intent: May not reflect specific prescriber instructions or rationale
  • New Medications: Recently approved drugs may have limited interaction data
  • Off-Label Use: Recommendations based on approved indications only

When to Seek Professional Advice

  • New Symptoms: Any unusual symptoms after starting or changing medications
  • Multiple Medications: Complex regimens with 5+ medications
  • Special Populations: Pregnancy, pediatric use, or significant kidney/liver disease
  • Conflicting Information: When app recommendations differ from prescriber instructions
  • Emergency Situations: Suspected adverse reactions or overdose
